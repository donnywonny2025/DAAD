# What Shows Up — Record Visibility Reference

This document tracks exactly what the hearing officer can and cannot see, what shows on each type of record, and what falls through the cracks.

---

## Two Separate Systems

Michigan maintains **two completely different databases**:

| System | Maintained By | What It Contains |
|---|---|---|
| **SOS Driving Record** | Secretary of State | Convictions, points, crashes, suspensions, revocations, reinstatements |
| **Criminal Record (ICHAT/LEIN)** | Michigan State Police | Arrests, charges, convictions, open cases, warrants |

The hearing officer at the OHA reviews your **driving record** — not your full criminal history. This is an administrative proceeding, not a criminal trial.

---

## What DOES Show on the SOS Driving Record

| Item | Shows? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Traffic **convictions** | ✅ Yes | Both civil infractions and criminal traffic offenses |
| Points from moving violations | ✅ Yes | Stay on record 2 years from conviction date |
| License suspensions/revocations | ✅ Yes | Including the original OUIL revocations |
| Traffic crashes | ✅ Yes | Crash reports are filed |
| Failure to Appear (FAC) | ✅ Yes | Triggers automatic suspension |
| Failure to Comply w/ Judgment (FCJ) | ✅ Yes | Triggers automatic suspension |
| Out-of-state convictions | ⚠️ Maybe | MI is NOT a member of the Driver License Compact — doesn't auto-report interstate. But serious offenses may still be communicated. |

## What DOES NOT Show on the SOS Driving Record

| Item | Shows? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Dismissed charges** | ❌ No | No conviction = no abstract submitted to SOS |
| **Not guilty verdicts** | ❌ No | Acquittal doesn't generate an abstract |
| **Pending/open cases** | ❌ Generally no | Unless they triggered a license suspension (like a bench warrant for failure to appear) |
| **Arrests without conviction** | ❌ No | Arrests that don't result in conviction don't hit the driving record |
| **Adjudication withheld** (FL term) | ❌ No | If Florida withheld adjudication, there's no conviction to report |
| **Reduced charges** (non-abstractable) | ❌ No | If plea-bargained down to a non-moving violation, no abstract |

## What the Hearing Officer Actually Has Access To

The OHA hearing officer works from:

1. **Your certified Michigan driving record** — this is the core document
2. **The evidence packet YOU submitted** — SOS-258, drug screen, support letters
3. **Your live testimony** — what you say under questioning
4. **NDR/PDPS pointers** — interstate flags from other states

They are NOT running an ICHAT criminal background check on you at the hearing. Their job is to evaluate the "clear and convincing evidence" standard for sobriety — not to investigate your full life history.

---

## Critical Nuances

### The "Abstracting" Mechanism
A court only sends an "abstract" (record entry) to the SOS when there is a **conviction** or a **finding of responsibility** for a civil infraction. If a case is:
- Dismissed → no abstract
- Found not guilty → no abstract
- Reduced to non-abstractable offense → no abstract
- Still pending/open → no conviction yet → generally no abstract

### Michigan is NOT in the Driver License Compact
Michigan opted out of the interstate agreement where states automatically share ALL traffic convictions. This means:
- Minor out-of-state tickets may never hit your MI record
- Serious offenses (DUI-related) are more likely to be communicated
- The NDR/PDPS catches revocations and suspensions, but not necessarily minor infractions

### Florida "Adjudication Withheld"
Florida commonly uses "adjudication withheld" — where a judge finds you guilty but withholds formal adjudication (no conviction on record). If FL withheld adjudication on something:
- It is NOT a conviction
- It does NOT generate an abstract to Michigan
- It would NOT appear on a Michigan driving record

---

## Bottom Line for Strategy

Before filing, we need to pull records from all three states to see exactly what's there. But the key takeaway is:

**Only convictions and licensing actions show on the SOS driving record that the hearing officer reviews. Dismissed cases, pending matters, arrests without conviction, and adjudication-withheld dispositions do NOT appear.**

If something from 3 years ago was never charged, was dismissed, is still pending, or was resolved without a conviction — it likely does not exist on the record the hearing officer will be reviewing.
