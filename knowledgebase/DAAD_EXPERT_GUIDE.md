# DAAD / OHA License Restoration Expert Guide (Michigan)

> [!CAUTION]
> The burden of proof is entirely on you. You must provide **"clear and convincing evidence"** that your substance abuse problem is under control and likely to remain under control. Discrepancies in your paperwork will result in an automatic denial, and you can only apply once a year.

## 1. Substance Use Evaluation (Form SOS-258)
This is the single most important document of your appeal. It must be completed by a qualified, licensed professional.

- **90-Day Window**: The evaluation is only valid for 90 days from the date the evaluator signs it to the date OHA receives it.
- **Content Requirements**: It must exhaustively detail:
  - Your complete lifetime treatment history (inpatient, outpatient).
  - Every single substance-related incident/conviction, including non-driving incidents.
  - A highly specific relapse history (dates of abstinence, dates of relapse).
  - Your current support group tracking (e.g., AA or NA attendance).
  - A definitive diagnostic impression (DSM-5) and a prognosis (must be highly favorable).
- **Absolute Consistency**: The dates you tell the evaluator regarding your last use of drugs/alcohol **must perfectly match** your drug screen and your letters of support. Not off by a month. Exactly the same.

## 2. Laboratory 12-Panel Urinalysis
You cannot use an at-home or generic instant test. It **must** be a lab-certified 12-panel screen.

- **Integrity Variables required**: The test must detect dilution and tampering.
  - It must explicitly test for **Creatinine**, **Specific Gravity**, and **pH**. If these are missing, it's rejected.
- **Substances**: It must screen for amphetamines, barbiturates, benzodiazepines, cocaine, opiates, and marijuana.
- **Timing**: Like the Evaluation, this must be recent and ideally performed around the same time as your SOS-258.

## 3. Reference/Support Letters 
You are required to submit a minimum of 3 but no more than 6 letters from people who know you well.

> [!WARNING]
> Form letters, templates, or copied paragraphs are immediately rejected. Every letter must be written organically by the author.

**Every single letter MUST:**
1. Be original, dated, and strictly **notarized**.
2. Include the author's complete contact information (address, phone number).
3. Explain exactly how the person knows you, how long they've known you, and how often they see you.
4. Include detailed knowledge of your past substance abuse and your current recovery routine.
5. **The Golden Rule**: The author MUST explicitly state the physical date of the *last time they personally saw or knew you to use drugs or alcohol*. If one letter says "August 2021" and your Substance Abuse Eval says "July 2021", you lose.

## 4. Best Practices for the Hearing
- Have at least **12-18 months** of strict, provable continuous sobriety before applying. 
- You will be aggressively cross-examined by the hearing officer. Prepare your timeline meticulously.
- If you were required to use a BAIID (Ignition Interlock), you must pull the "Final Report" demonstrating no significant violations or tampers.
