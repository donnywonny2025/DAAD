# DAAD Legal Strategy & Precedent Breakdown

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The burden of proof in an OHA/DAAD hearing operates differently than criminal court. You are essentially assumed "guilty" of being a risk, and it is 100% on you to prove by "clear and convincing evidence" that you are safe. 

## Why Do People Get Denied?

1. **Document Discrepancies**: If your Substance Abuse Evaluator writes that your last drink was "July 15, 2021" but your sister's notarized support letter says "He hasn't drank since August 2021," you **will be denied immediately**. The state cross-references every single date. 
2. **The "Special Occasion" Trap**: Michigan law requires absolute, lifetime abstinence for license restoration. If you state or imply that you only drink on "special occasions" (like a champagne toast at a wedding), you fail.
3. **Weak Support Letters**: Letters that say "He is a good guy and needs to drive for work" are thrown out. The state does not care about your hardship or your job. They only care about your sobriety.
4. **Premature Filing**: Filing before you have at least 12-18 months of proven sobriety leaves an incredibly weak record. 
5. **Gaps in Testimony**: In the actual hearing, if the officer asks you a question that contradicts your submitted paperwork, you will lose credibility and be denied.

## Pro Se (No Lawyer) vs. Representation

### Going Without a Lawyer (Pro Se)
- **Pros**: You save typical retainer fees ($3,000 - $6,000).
- **Cons**: The statistics are heavily stacked against you. Hearers strictly enforce Michigan Administrative codes. If you say the wrong trigger word, fail to properly format your 12-panel lab report, or submit letters that aren't strictly notarized, they deny you. 
- **The Punishment**: If you are denied, you are **locked out for a full calendar year**. Worse, everything you submitted becomes a permanent record. If you hire a lawyer the next year, they have to fight against the mistakes you made on the record the year prior.

### Getting a Lawyer
- **Pros**: Lawyers handle the burden of proof. They physically prepare you for cross-examination by the hearing officer. They vet the Substance Abuse Evaluation before it is sent to ensure the evaluator used the correct DSM-5 terminology, and they reject/edit your friend's support letters so they meet the state evidentiary standard.
- **Cons**: High upfront cost.

## How to Get Your Master Driving Record

To know exactly what the OHA is looking at, you must purchase your own Master Driving Record from the state. 
- **Where**: You can purchase it directly from the Michigan Secretary of State online portal, or by submitting form BDVR-153.
- **Why**: Your lawyer (or you) needs this to ensure your reported conviction dates perfectly match what the state already knows.
