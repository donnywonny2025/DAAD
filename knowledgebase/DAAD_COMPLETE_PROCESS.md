# Michigan DAAD / OHA — Complete Process Guide
## Scraped from michigan.gov/sos + legal research sources — April 2026

---

## 1. Overview

The **Office of Hearings and Administrative Oversight (OHAO)** — formerly referred to as the Driver Assessment and Appeal Division (DAAD) — handles all license restoration hearings for the Michigan Secretary of State.

**Burden of Proof:** The petitioner must demonstrate by **"clear and convincing evidence"** that:
- Their alcohol/substance abuse problem is under control (minimum 6-12 months sobriety)
- Their problem is **likely to remain** under control (lifetime commitment)

## 2. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Order Your Michigan Certified Driving Record
- Go to **MiLogin** at [milogin.michigan.gov](https://milogin.michigan.gov)
- Request a **certified complete driving record** — look for **"42" in the top-left corner**
- Review the section **below "End of Record History"** for:
  - Active barriers/suspensions
  - Your **"eligible for review" date** — you cannot file until this date passes

### Step 2: Prepare Required Documentation
All documents must be submitted as a **single unified packet**. Partial submissions are rejected.

#### Form SOS-257 — Request for Hearing
- This is your formal petition to the Secretary of State
- All information is sworn true under penalty of perjury
- Download: [OHAO Form Package (SOS-257 + SOS-258)](https://www.michigan.gov/sos/-/media/Project/Websites/sos/sos/OHAO_Form_Package_SOS_257_258.pdf)

#### Form SOS-258 — Substance Abuse Evaluation
- Must be completed by a **qualified/licensed substance abuse evaluator**
- ⚠️ **CRITICAL: Must be dated WITHIN 90 DAYS of your submission date**
- The evaluator must document:
  - Full history of charges and convictions
  - Treatment history
  - Sobriety date and supporting evidence
  - Clinical assessment of relapse risk

#### 12-Panel Urinalysis (Drug Screen)
- **MUST be lab-certified** — no instant/rapid "cup" tests accepted
- **MUST include Integrity Variables:**
  - Creatinine level
  - Specific Gravity
  - pH level
- These prove the sample wasn't diluted or tampered with
- Chain-of-custody documentation required

#### Community Support Letters (3-6 Required)
- **Must be notarized** with original signatures
- Each letter MUST include:
  - How the writer knows you and for how long
  - Their knowledge of your past substance use
  - Verification of your **exact sobriety date**
  - Their observations of your current sober lifestyle
  - Their belief that you will remain sober
- ⚠️ **DO NOT use template/identical language across letters** — will cause automatic denial
- ⚠️ **DO NOT include phrases like "occasional drink" or "social drinker"** — instant red flag

### Step 3: File via DAIS
- **DAIS = Driver Appeals Integrated System**
- Requires a **MiLogin account** with identity verification (smartphone camera)
- Upload all documents as a unified packet
- System confirms receipt and schedules hearing

### Step 4: Attend the Hearing
- Hearings are conducted **virtually via Microsoft Teams**
- You will face:
  - A **Hearing Officer** (reviews evidence, asks questions)
  - An **Attorney representing the Secretary of State** (cross-examines you)
- You (or your attorney) present testimony regarding:
  - Sobriety history and timeline
  - Rehabilitation efforts
  - Current lifestyle
  - Commitment to safe driving
- ⚠️ **Inconsistencies between your testimony and documents = denial**

### Step 5: Decision
- Written decision issued after hearing
- **If Approved:** Restricted license with BAIID (Ignition Interlock) for minimum 1 year
  - You are responsible for ALL violations on the device, even if someone else drives
  - Must pay reinstatement fees
  - May need to complete vision/knowledge/road tests
- **If Denied:** You may appeal to Circuit Court within **63 days** (up to 182 days with good cause)

## 3. Do I Need an Attorney?

Per the Michigan SOS website: **"You are not required to have an attorney."** 

However, the process is adversarial. The state has an attorney cross-examining you. Common pro se mistakes include:
- Document date mismatches (SOS-258 outside 90-day window)
- Reference letters with identical/template language
- Admitting to "occasional" or "social" use during testimony
- Incomplete drug screen (missing integrity variables)
- Filing before the eligible-for-review date

## 4. Multi-State Records (NDR / PDPS)

### What is the NDR?
The **National Driver Register (NDR)** maintains the **Problem Driver Pointer System (PDPS)** — a federal database that flags individuals whose license has been revoked, suspended, canceled, or denied in ANY state.

### Why It Matters for Jeff
- Lived in **Michigan, Florida, and Arizona**
- Any actions in FL or AZ will show as "pointers" in the NDR
- The hearing officer WILL check for multi-state issues
- Better to proactively pull your own NDR record to identify surprises

### How to Self-Request NDR/PDPS
**Option 1: Electronic Request**
- Website: [nhtsa.gov/content/ndr](https://www.nhtsa.gov/content/ndr)

**Option 2: Mail-in Request (Notarized)**
Include:
- Full legal name, DOB, sex
- Driver license number + issuing state
- Height, weight, eye color
- Notarized signature OR perjury declaration
- Mail to:
  **National Driver Register**
  1200 New Jersey Avenue, S.E.
  Washington, D.C. 20590

### State-Specific Records (if NDR insufficient)
- **Florida:** Request through [Florida DHSMV](https://www.flhsmv.gov/driver-licenses-id-cards/driving-record-check/)
- **Arizona:** Request through [Arizona MVD](https://azmvdnow.gov/)
- **Michigan:** Request through [MiLogin](https://milogin.michigan.gov/)

## 5. Key Dates & Deadlines

| Requirement | Deadline |
|---|---|
| SOS-258 Substance Eval | Within 90 days of submission |
| Circuit Court Appeal (if denied) | Within 63 days of decision |
| Extended Appeal Window | Up to 182 days with good cause |
| BAIID Interlock (if approved) | Minimum 1 year |

---

## 6. Common Denial Reasons

1. **Date Mismatches** — Sobriety date on SOS-258 doesn't match reference letters or testimony
2. **"Special Occasion" Admission** — Saying you drink on holidays or special occasions
3. **Template Letters** — Using identical wording across multiple reference letters
4. **Premature Filing** — Filing before the eligible-for-review date
5. **Missing Integrity Variables** — Drug screen without Creatinine / Specific Gravity / pH
6. **Incomplete Evaluation** — SOS-258 missing required clinical details
7. **Inconsistent Testimony** — Contradicting what's written in your documents
