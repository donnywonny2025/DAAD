# Case Law Research Plan
## Targeted Legal Precedent for Jeff Kerr's OHA Appeal

---

## Research Objective
Find specific Michigan case law and appellate decisions that directly support Jeff's position at the OHA hearing. Not general DUI law — specific restoration/appeal decisions with fact patterns similar to Jeff's.

---

## Priority Research Tracks

### Track 1: "Old Offense, Modern Petitioner"
**What I'm looking for:** Cases where petitioners with pre-2003 OUIL offenses successfully won license restoration years or decades later.
**Why it matters:** Proves the hearing officer framework accommodates people in Jeff's exact situation — old offenses under old law, extended sobriety.
**Search targets:**
- Michigan Court of Appeals decisions on SOS/DAAD/OHA appeals
- Circuit court reversals where old offense dates were at issue
- Any decision referencing the OUIL → OWI transition as relevant context

### Track 2: "Length of Sobriety as Decisive Factor"
**What I'm looking for:** Cases where 10+ years of sobriety was cited as strong evidence of "likely to remain under control."
**Why it matters:** Jeff has 25+ years since the incidents. If case law shows that extended sobriety is the strongest indicator, that's Jeff's trump card.
**Search targets:**
- Court of Appeals language on what constitutes "clear and convincing" evidence of control
- Decisions where hearing officers were reversed for ignoring long sobriety history
- Any discussion of whether a "minimum" sobriety period (1 year) vs. decades-long sobriety is weighed differently

### Track 3: "Multi-State License / Out-of-State Driving"
**What I'm looking for:** Cases where petitioners held licenses in other states while revoked in Michigan, and how hearing officers/courts treated it.
**Why it matters:** The Florida license is a known variable. Need to know if it's been a death sentence for others or a non-issue.
**Search targets:**
- DAAD/OHA decisions involving dual-state licensing
- Circuit court appeals where FL/AZ/other state license was discussed
- Any language on whether clean out-of-state driving record SUPPORTS the petition

### Track 4: "Pro Se Success Patterns"
**What I'm looking for:** Cases where self-represented petitioners won at the OHA level or on circuit court appeal.
**Why it matters:** Jeff is going pro se. Need to identify what successful pro se petitioners did differently.
**Search targets:**
- Court of Appeals decisions mentioning pro se petitioners
- Common deficiencies in pro se cases that led to denial (so we can avoid them)
- Any patterns in successful pro se evidence packets

### Track 5: "Abuse of Discretion / Arbitrary Denial"
**What I'm looking for:** Cases where circuit courts overturned OHA denials as arbitrary, capricious, or unsupported by evidence.
**Why it matters:** If Jeff gets denied, understanding what grounds have worked on appeal tells us how to build the record NOW.
**Search targets:**
- MCL 257.323 circuit court reversals
- "Not supported by competent, material, and substantial evidence" holdings
- Cases where the hearing officer ignored favorable evidence

### Track 6: "Legislative Intent / Policy Arguments"
**What I'm looking for:** Any court decision or legislative history acknowledging that the old OUIL framework was overly punitive or that modern reforms reflect changed policy.
**Why it matters:** While Rule 13 says hardship alone doesn't matter, legislative intent showing the state WANTS people like Jeff rehabilitated could influence the hearing officer's discretion.
**Search targets:**
- Legislative committee records on MCL 257.304 (Sobriety Court)
- Court of Appeals language on the purpose/intent of license restoration
- Any judicial opinions referencing reform as a correction of past over-punishment

## Research Methods (Ordered by Reliability)

1. **Google Scholar — Case Law search** (michigan.scholar.google.com)
   - Direct access to published Michigan Court of Appeals opinions
   - Can filter by date, court level, and keyword
   - FREE — no Westlaw/LexisNexis needed

2. **Michigan Court of Appeals opinions database** (courts.michigan.gov)
   - Official published/unpublished opinions
   - Searchable by case name, topic, statute

3. **Casetext / Google Scholar backlinks**
   - Free case law databases with citation tracking
   - Can find cases that CITE key statutes (MCL 257.303, 257.304, 257.323)

4. **Defense attorney blogs** (secondary source — confirms which cases matter)
   - Randa Law, Barone Defense, Grabel Law all publish case analyses
   - Good for identifying landmark decisions without reading 50 opinions

5. **Firecrawl / Browser scraping** (for paywalled or hard-to-reach content)
   - Last resort for content behind basic access walls
